# Talkie Content Style Guide

## Voice
- **Confident but accessible** - Expert, not salesy
- **Practical over theoretical** - Show, don't just tell
- **First-person when appropriate** - "I've found..." vs "One should..."
- **Direct** - Short sentences, clear value

## Tone
- **Helpful** - genuinely useful, not promotional
- **Technical but approachable** - Assume curiosity, not expertise
- **Honest** - Acknowledge tradeoffs and limitations

## Formatting
- **Headers:** Clear hierarchy (H1 → H2 → H3)
- **Lists:** Bullets for parallel items, numbered for sequences
- **Code blocks:** Use for templates, JSON, examples
- **Bold:** Key terms, CTAs, critical warnings

## Word Choice
| Use | Avoid |
|-----|-------|
| "You" | "The user" |
| "Try this:" | "It is recommended to:" |
| "Download Talkie" | "Leverage the Talkie solution" |
| "When this fails" | "Potential failure modes include" |

## Conventions
- **Product name:** Talkie (not "Talkie app" or "Talkie software")
- **Features:** lowercase (voice workflows, not "Voice Workflows")
- **URLs:** /guides/, /docs/, /templates/, /engineering/, /integrations/
- **CTA placement:** End of each article + one natural placement mid-content

## Length Guidelines
- Guides: 1500-2500 words
- Comparisons: 1000-1500 words
- Templates: 500-1000 words + downloadable content
- Engineering docs: 1000-2000 words
